32 WHEREAS, the Vietnam War was fought in the Republic of Vietnam from
33 1955 to 1975 and involved regular forces from the Democratic Republic of
34 Vietnam and Viet Cong guerilla forces in armed conflict with the United
35 States Armed Forces, the armed forces of allies of the United States, and the
36 armed forces of the Republic of Vietnam; and
*ADF146*

1
2 WHEREAS, the United States Armed Forces became involved in Vietnam
3 because the United States Government wanted to provide direct military
4 support to the government of the Republic of Vietnam to defend against the
5 growing threat of communism from the Democratic Republic of Vietnam; and
6
7 WHEREAS, citizens of Arkansas answered their country's call as they
8 have in every armed conflict in which the United States has been a
9 participant; and
10
11 WHEREAS, some Arkansans served in or off the coast of the Republic of
12 Vietnam, while others served in critical roles such as training and
13 logistics; and
14
15 WHEREAS, more than fifty-eight thousand (58,000) members of the United
16 States Armed Forces lost their lives in the Vietnam War, including five
17 hundred ninety-two (592) Arkansans; and
18
19 WHEREAS, in 1982, the Vietnam Veterans Memorial was dedicated in
20 Washington, D.C., to commemorate the members of the United States Armed
21 Forces who died or were declared missing in action in Vietnam; and
22
23 WHEREAS, the Arkansas Vietnam Veterans Memorial is inscribed with the
24 names of Arkansans who lost their lives in the Vietnam War, and the memorial
25 was dedicated on the grounds of the State Capitol in Little Rock on March 7,
26 1987, by retired United States Army General William Westmoreland; and
27
28 WHEREAS, the Vietnam War was an extremely divisive issue among the
29 people of the United States and a conflict that caused a generation of
30 veterans to have to wait too long for the public of the United States to
31 acknowledge and honor their efforts and services; and
32
33 WHEREAS, members of the United States Armed Forces who bravely and
34 faithfully served the United States during the Vietnam War were often wrongly
35 criticized for the decisions of policymakers that were beyond the control of
36 those servicemen and servicewomen; and
2 04-01-2025 10:28:11 ADF146
As Engrossed: H4/1/25 HR1090
1
2 WHEREAS, recognizing March 29, 2025, as Vietnam War Veterans Day and
3 March 2025 as Vietnam Era Veterans Month in Arkansas is an appropriate way to
4 honor the members of the United States Armed Forces who served in Vietnam;
5 and
6
7 WHEREAS, it is important for all Arkansans to express appreciation for
8 the veterans of the Vietnam War, to demonstrate a resolve that the people of
9 the State of Arkansas shall never forget the sacrifices and service of a
10 generation of veterans who served in the Vietnam War, and to promote
11 awareness of the faithful service and contributions of the veterans of the
12 Vietnam War era,
13
14 NOW THEREFORE,
15 BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE NINETY-FIFTH GENERAL
16 ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF ARKANSAS:
17
18 THAT the House of Representatives recognize March 29, 2025, as Vietnam
19 War Veterans Day and March 2025 as Vietnam Era Veterans Month in Arkansas in
20 appreciation of the sacrifices and contributions made by Vietnam War
21 veterans.
