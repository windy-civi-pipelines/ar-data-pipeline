16 TO HONOR DELTA SIGMA THETA SORORITY,
17 INC., FOR ITS ROLE IN THE 1913 WOMAN
18 SUFFRAGE PROCESSION AND THE CENTENNIAL
19 CELEBRATION MARCH, AND ITS ADVOCACY FOR
20 THE ADVANCEMENT OF WOMEN.
21
22 WHEREAS, Delta Sigma Theta Sorority, Inc., was founded in 1913 by
23 twenty-two (22) women at Howard University in Washington, D.C.; and
24
25 WHEREAS, the founders of Delta Sigma Theta Sorority, Inc., established
26 the sorority in order to address issues such as public service, women's
27 advancement, and social activism; and
28
29 WHEREAS, today, Delta Sigma Theta Sorority, Inc., is one of four (4)
30 college sororities for African American women, and is the largest African
31 American Greek-letter sorority in the world; and
32
33 WHEREAS, since its founding, Delta Sigma Theta Sorority, Inc., has had
34 more than three hundred fifty thousand (350,000) women members of all racial
35 and ethnic backgrounds; and
36

1 WHEREAS, Delta Sigma Theta Sorority, Inc., currently has over one
2 thousand (1,000) collegiate and alumnae chapters located across the world;
3 and
4
5 WHEREAS, Delta Sigma Theta Sorority, Inc.'s community service and
6 social and philanthropic projects revolve around the "Five-Point Programmatic
7 Thrust" which encompasses economic development, educational development,
8 international awareness and involvement, physical and mental health, and
9 political awareness and involvement; and
10
11 WHEREAS, on March 3, 1913, the newly founded Delta Sigma Theta
12 Sorority, Inc., performed its first public act of service by marching in cap
13 and gown in the Woman Suffrage Procession down Pennsylvania Avenue in
14 Washington, D.C., despite opposition by some to the presence of African
15 American women in the parade; and
16
17 WHEREAS, the 1913 Woman Suffrage Procession became one of the pivotal
18 events helping to solidify the passage of the Nineteenth Amendment to the
19 United States Constitution seven (7) years later, which gave women the right
20 to vote; and
21
22 WHEREAS, on Sunday, March 3, 2013, several thousand members of Delta
23 Sigma Theta Sorority, Inc., commemorated the one hundredth anniversary of the
24 1913 Woman Suffrage Procession and the role the organization's founders
25 played by recreating the original five-thousand-person march down
26 Pennsylvania Avenue in Washington, D.C.,
27
28 NOW THEREFORE,
29 BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE NINETY-FIFTH GENERAL
30 ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF ARKANSAS:
31
32 THAT the House of Representatives honor Delta Sigma Theta Sorority,
33 Inc., for its role in the Woman Suffrage Procession and the centennial
34 celebration march, and its advocacy for the advancement of women.
35
36 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED THAT upon adoption, a copy of this resolution be
