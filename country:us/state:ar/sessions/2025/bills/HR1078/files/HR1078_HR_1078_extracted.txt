16 TO COMMEND ALPHA PHI ALPHA FRATERNITY,
17 INC., FOR ONE HUNDRED EIGHTEEN (118)
18 YEARS OF OUTSTANDING SERVICE AND
19 LEADERSHIP IN ITS COMMUNITIES.
20
21 WHEREAS, Alpha Phi Alpha Fraternity, Inc., is the first and oldest
22 intercollegiate Greek-letter fraternity established for African Americans;
23 and
24
25 WHEREAS, Alpha Phi Alpha Fraternity, Inc., was founded on December 4,
26 1906, on the campus of Cornell University in Ithaca, New York, by students
27 who recognized the need for a strong bond of brotherhood among African
28 descendants in this country; and
29
30 WHEREAS, Alpha Phi Alpha Fraternity, Inc., was founded by visionaries,
31 known as the "jewels" of the fraternity, including Henry Arthur Callis,
32 Charles Henry Chapman, Eugene Kinckle Jones, George Biddle Kelley, Nathaniel
33 Allison Murray, Robert Harold Ogle, and Vertner Woodson Tandy; and
34
35 WHEREAS, Alpha Phi Alpha Fraternity, Inc., initially served as a study
36 and support group for minority students who faced racial prejudice both

3 WHEREAS, the Jewel founders and early leaders of Alpha Phi Alpha
4 Fraternity, Inc., succeeded in laying a firm foundation for the fraternity's
5 principles of scholarship, fellowship, good character, and the uplifting of
6 humanity; and
7
8 WHEREAS, chapters of Alpha Phi Alpha Fraternity, Inc., have served
9 communities and campuses throughout Arkansas since 1926; and
10
11 WHEREAS, Alpha Phi Alpha Fraternity, Inc., continues to proliferate,
12 spanning the globe with nearly eight hundred (800) chapters and two hundred
13 thousand (200,000) members located throughout the United States, Canada,
14 various Caribbean islands, Africa, the West Indies, Europe, and Asia; and
15
16 WHEREAS, Alpha Phi Alpha Fraternity, Inc., continues to stress academic
17 excellence among its members and recognizes the educational, economic,
18 political, and social challenges faced by African Americans nationally and
19 internationally; and
20
21 WHEREAS, Alpha Phi Alpha Fraternity, Inc., participates in Project
22 Alpha, Go-To-High-School, Go-To-College, Scouting America, and A Voteless
23 People is a Hopeless People programs in response to those challenges; and
24
25 WHEREAS, the Alpha Phi Alpha Fraternity, Inc., motto is "First of All,
26 Servants of All, We Shall Transcend All"; and
27
28 WHEREAS, members of Alpha Phi Alpha Fraternity, Inc., include many
29 legendary civil rights leaders such as the Reverend Dr. Martin Luther King,
30 Jr., National Association for the Advancement of Colored People founder
31 W.E.B. DuBois, civic leader John Mack, Reverend Joseph E. Lowery, and
32 Reverend C.T. Vivian; and

5 WHEREAS, Alpha Phi Alpha Fraternity, Inc., was directly responsible for
