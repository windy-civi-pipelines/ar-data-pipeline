17 TO SUPPORT ARKANSAS'S ENGAGING THE
18 JUSTICE COUNTS METRICS AND TO URGE
19 POLICYMAKERS THROUGHOUT STATE AND LOCAL
20 GOVERNMENT ENTITIES TO IMPLEMENT AND
21 UTILIZE THE JUSTICE COUNTS METRICS.
22
23 WHEREAS, Justice Counts is an initiative led by the United States
24 Department of Justice's Office of Justice Programs' Bureau of Justice
25 Assistance, The Council of State Governments' Justice Center, and over
26 nineteen (19) partners to develop and help implement consensus-driven metrics
27 for criminal justice agencies; and
28
29 WHEREAS, the Justice Counts metrics are developed in partnership with a
30 wide range of experts across the criminal justice system; and
31
32 WHEREAS, Arkansas policymakers are often forced to make critical
33 decisions about the safety of their constituents using limited or out-of-date
34 criminal justice data; and
35
36 WHEREAS, Arkansas consistently ranks among the top five (5) states with
*ADF074*

1 the highest violent crime rates in the United States; and
2
3 WHEREAS, Arkansas consistently ranks among the top five (5) states with
4 the highest incarceration rates in the United States; and
5
6 WHEREAS, Arkansas consistently ranks among the states with the highest
7 rates of those leaving incarceration to be re-incarcerated within three (3)
8 years; and
9
10 WHEREAS, people in Arkansas on probation or parole account for
11 approximately sixteen percent (16%) of violent crime arrests and
12 approximately sixty-two percent (62%) of prison admissions; and
13
14 WHEREAS, Justice Counts would help to remedy this by ensuring decision
15 makers have access to consistent, timely, and accurate data across the
16 criminal justice system; and
17
18 WHEREAS, twenty-nine (29) states and two (2) territories, including states
19 such as Georgia, Indiana, Kentucky, Louisiana, Missouri, North Carolina, and
20 Tennessee, have committed to implementing the Justice Counts metrics,
21
22 NOW THEREFORE,
23 BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE NINETY-FIFTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE
24 STATE OF ARKANSAS:
25
26 THAT the Senate support Arkansas's engaging the Justice Counts metrics
27 and urge policymakers throughout state and local government entities to
28 implement and utilize the metrics and commit to making the latest criminal
29 justice data available.
30
