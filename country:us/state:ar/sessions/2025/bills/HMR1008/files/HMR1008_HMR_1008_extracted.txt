15 TO REMEMBER BOXER TOMMY MORRISON OF
16 GRAVETTE FOR HIS LEGENDARY BOXING SKILLS
17 THAT BROUGHT RENOWN TO HIM AND TO THE
18 STATE OF ARKANSAS.
19
20 WHEREAS, Tommy "The Duke" Morrison, formerly of Omaha, Nebraska, passed
21 away on September 1, 2013, at the age of forty-four (44); and
22
23 WHEREAS, Tommy "The Duke" Morrison was born in Gravette, Arkansas, on
24 January 2, 1969, to a mother, Diana, of Native American ancestry and a
25 father, Tim, of Scottish ancestry; and
26
27 WHEREAS, Tommy "The Duke" Morrison spent his teenage years in Oklahoma,
28 and his father encouraged him to take up boxing during the 1970s, a sport in
29 which Morrison found immediate success; and
30
31 WHEREAS, Tommy "The Duke" Morrison acquired his nickname, "The Duke",
32 based on a claim that he was a distant relative of famed actor Marion "John
33 Wayne" Morrison, who was also nicknamed "The Duke"; and
34
35 WHEREAS, after graduating from high school, Tommy "The Duke" Morrison
36 received a football scholarship to Emporia State University, and in the same

1 year won the Regional Heavy Weight Title, Kansas City Golden Gloves, and
2 advanced to the National Golden Gloves in Omaha, Nebraska, where he won the
3 majority of his fights; and
4
5 WHEREAS, also in that same year, Tommy "The Duke" Morrison took part in
6 the Western Olympic trials, qualified for the nationals, and garnered the
7 tournament's Outstanding Fighter award; and
8
9 WHEREAS, Tommy "The Duke" Morrison had accumulated numerous wins as a
10 boxer when he was spotted in 1989 by actor Sylvester Stallone, who, after
11 watching one of Morrison's bouts, arranged a script reading and cast Morrison
12 in the film "Rocky V" as Tommy "The Machine" Gunn, a young and talented
13 protege of retired main character Rocky Balboa; and
14
15 WHEREAS, among his many victories, Tommy "The Duke" Morrison became the
16 new World Boxing Organization heavyweight champion in 1993 by defeating
17 heavyweight boxing legend George Foreman; and
18
19 WHEREAS, Tommy "The Duke" Morrison's professional boxing record
20 encompassed fifty-two (52) fights, of which he won forty-eight and lost three
21 (48-3), and his amateur boxing record encompassed two hundred twenty-two
22 fights, of which he won two hundred two and lost twenty (202-20); and
23
24 WHEREAS, the World Boxing Organization said Tommy "The Duke" Morrison
25 would be remembered best for his dangerous punching power and especially his
26 left hook, and two-time heavyweight champion Pinklon Thomas ranked Morrison
27 ahead of Mike Tyson as the hardest puncher he had ever faced,
28
29 NOW THEREFORE,
30 BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE NINETY-FIFTH GENERAL
31 ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF ARKANSAS:
32
33 THAT the House of Representatives remember boxer Tommy Morrison of
34 Gravette for his legendary boxing skills that brought renown to him and to
35 the State of Arkansas.
