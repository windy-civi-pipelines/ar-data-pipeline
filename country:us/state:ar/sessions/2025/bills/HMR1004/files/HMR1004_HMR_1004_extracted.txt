11 IN RESPECTFUL MEMORY OF FORMER ARKANSAS ATTORNEY

25 WHEREAS, former Arkansas Attorney General, United States Congressman,
26 and forty-third Governor of Arkansas James "Jim" Guy Tucker, formerly of
27 Little Rock, passed away Thursday, February 13, 2025, at the age of eighty-
28 one (81); and
29
30 WHEREAS, former Governor Tucker was born June 13, 1943, in Oklahoma
31 City, Oklahoma, as one (1) of three (3) children, and in 1945 the family
32 moved back to Arkansas where his parents had been born; and
33
34 WHEREAS, former Governor Tucker was raised in a modest brick home on
35 Stonewall Road in Little Rock in a neighborhood today known as the Heights,
36 and where Tucker once recalled having seen horses and buggies tethered to a

1 "hitchin' rail" in front of the local hardware store; and
2
3 WHEREAS, former Governor Tucker's paternal grandfather was the city
4 marshal in El Dorado, his maternal grandfather was the White County Judge,
5 and his father was a World War I veteran and an Arkansas state auditor and
6 later manager of the state's Social Security system, and his mother was a
7 college graduate who owned Merle Norman Cosmetics studios in central Arkansas
8 in order to pay for her son's college education; and
9
10 WHEREAS, other formative experiences for former Governor Tucker
11 included becoming an Eagle Scout, learning to play guitar, being a pole
12 vaulter and a football player, and becoming involved in student affairs; and
13
14 WHEREAS, former Governor Tucker was awoken from sleep on the night of
15 March 31, 1960, when a B-47 bomber exploded in the air over Little Rock,
16 killing three (3) crew members in addition to two (2) fatalities on the
17 ground as the wreckage landed in the Hillcrest neighborhood, and this made a
18 deep impression on the teenaged Tucker; and
19
20 WHEREAS, former Governor Tucker attended Harvard University in Boston,
21 Massachusetts, beginning in the autumn of 1961, a time during which Tucker
22 was also diagnosed with the ulcerative colitis that would, years later, lead
23 to colon cancer and the need for a liver transplant; and
24
25 WHEREAS, during the summer of 1963, former Governor Tucker worked for
26 United States Senator John McClellan in Washington, D.C., and that summer
27 Tucker met United States Attorney General Robert Kennedy and watched the
28 Reverend Martin Luther King, Jr., give his famous "I Have a Dream" speech on
29 the steps of the Lincoln Memorial; and
30
31 WHEREAS, former Governor Tucker graduated in the spring of 1964 from
32 Harvard University with a bachelor's degree in government, then immediately
33 joined the United States Marines, but he was discharged within three (3)
34 months because of his increasingly serious health issues, so Tucker turned
35 his focus to helping former Governor Winthrop "Win" Rockefeller during
36 Rockefeller's campaign for governor; and

1
2 WHEREAS, in 1965 and 1967, former Governor Tucker went to Vietnam to
3 cover the war as a journalist, corresponding with ABC News and several
4 Arkansas newspapers, including the Arkansas Democrat; and
5
6 WHEREAS, in 1968, former Governor Tucker graduated from the University
7 of Arkansas at Fayetteville School of Law, having earned his Juris Doctorate
8 while taking some time to teach at American University in Beirut, and he then
9 went to work for the Rose Law Firm in Little Rock; and
10
11 WHEREAS, in March 1970, former Governor Tucker went undercover for
12 thirty-six (36) hours as a prisoner at the Cummins Unit at the request of the
13 Chair of the Board of Corrections and successfully uncovered elements of the
14 corruption that was rumored to exist there at that time; and
15
16 WHEREAS, later in 1970, former Governor Tucker won the office of
17 prosecuting attorney for Pulaski and Perry counties; and
18
19 WHEREAS, in 1972, former Governor Tucker was elected to the office of
20 Attorney General of Arkansas, where he formed a commission to create
21 Arkansas's criminal law; and
22
23 WHEREAS, in 1972, former Governor Tucker met teacher Betty Allen
24 Alworth, married her in 1975, and treated her two (2) children "as his own
25 children right from the get-go" according to his stepson, and he had two (2)
26 more children with Alworth; and
27
28 WHEREAS, in 1976, former Governor Tucker was elected to the United
29 States Congress and was appointed to the House Ways and Means Committee, the
30 Social Security Subcommittee, and a special task force on welfare reform; and
31
32 WHEREAS, former Governor Tucker returned to private practice in 1979
33 before running for Governor of Arkansas in 1982, losing to his personal
34 friend and political rival William Jefferson "Bill" Clinton; and
35
36 WHEREAS, in 1990, former Governor Tucker was elected Lieutenant

1 Governor, and then became acting Governor in 1991 as then-Governor William
2 Jefferson "Bill" Clinton began his ultimately successful campaign to become
3 President of the United States; and
4
5 WHEREAS, while former Governor Tucker was in office as Governor of the
6 State of Arkansas, he worked diligently to improve Arkansas's prison system,
7 signed into law the Arkansas Civil Rights Act of 1993, and sought to increase
8 money for education without raising taxes; and
9
10 WHEREAS, former Governor Tucker was reelected as Governor in 1994, and
11 Tucker eventually stepped down as Governor; and
12
13 WHEREAS, former Governor Michael Dale "Mike" Huckabee became Governor
14 after former Governor Tucker stepped down, and Huckabee said of Tucker, "I
15 will remember that we worked together when it was best for the state. . . I
16 had great respect for his overcoming many health challenges so he could
17 continue to serve the state he truly loved"; and
18
19 WHEREAS, former President of the United States William Jefferson "Bill"
20 Clinton said of former Governor Tucker, "Jim Guy was a good and honorable man
21 and a good citizen who bore life's setbacks, including his health challenges,
22 with dignity and determination"; and
23
24 WHEREAS, Governor Sarah Huckabee Sanders said of former Governor
25 Tucker, "Jim Guy was part of a generation of larger-than-life Arkansans who
26 dominated Arkansas's political life when I was growing up and had an
27 immeasurable impact on our state"; and
28
29 WHEREAS, Representative Tracy Steele worked on former Governor Tucker's
30 staff, and Representative Steele described former Governor Tucker as "a good
31 man to work for"; and
32
33 WHEREAS, former Governor Tucker was known for his charisma and
34 intelligence, both of which enabled him to work well with both parties during
35 his many years of service as a political leader; and
36
4 02/20/2025 1:56:34 PM ADF084
HMR1004
1 WHEREAS, while former Governor Tucker was committed to improving the
2 lives of all Arkansans, he was also well-known for being a devoted father,
3 grandfather, mentor, and friend; and
4
5 WHEREAS, former Governor Tucker is survived by his wife, Betty Allen
6 Tucker, stepson Lance Alworth, Jr., stepdaughter Kelly Driscoll, and
7 daughters Anna Ashton and Sarah Tucker,
8
9 NOW THEREFORE,
10 BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE NINETY-FIFTH GENERAL
11 ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF ARKANSAS:
12
13 THAT the House of Representatives remember former Arkansas Attorney
14 General, United States Congressman, and forty-third Governor of Arkansas
15 James "Jim" Guy Tucker for his service to the nation and to the State of
16 Arkansas.
17
18 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED THAT upon adoption, a copy of this resolution be
19 presented to the family of former Governor James "Jim" Guy Tucker by the
20 Chief Clerk of the House of Representatives.
