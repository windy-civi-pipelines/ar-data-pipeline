17
18 WHEREAS, Sidney Moncrief is one of the greatest basketball players and
19 community leaders in Arkansas history; and
20
21 WHEREAS, Sidney Moncrief was born in a housing project in rural western
22 Little Rock on September 21, 1957, and was raised by his mother on her modest
23 salary earned as a cleaning woman at Howard Johnson hotels; and
24
25 WHEREAS, despite prior desegregation orders and efforts, Little Rock
26 was still racially segregated in many ways while Sidney Moncrief was growing
27 up in the city; and
28
29 WHEREAS, Sidney Moncrief credits membership in a Boy Scouts of America
30 troop and hard work in his family's garden with helping him escape being
31 drawn into a life of crime in his rough neighborhood; and
32
33 WHEREAS, Sidney Moncrief began playing basketball in grade seven (7) at
34 Dunbar Junior High School, but did not make the varsity team until grade nine
35 (9) at Booker Junior High School; and
36

1 WHEREAS, Sidney Moncrief made the varsity team at Hall High School and,
2 as a senior, was named to several high school All-American teams; and
3
4 WHEREAS, Sidney Moncrief was recruited upon his high school graduation
5 by Louisiana State University, Arkansas State University, and the University
6 of Arkansas at Fayetteville, but he chose the University of Arkansas at
7 Fayetteville because of its new coach, Eddie Sutton, who was ushering in
8 novel ideas about how to play the game of basketball; and
9
10 WHEREAS, while the six-foot, four-inch (6'4") Sidney Moncrief played
11 basketball at the University of Arkansas at Fayetteville, his signature
12 playing style with team members Ron Brewer and Marvin Delph earned them the
13 moniker "The Triplets" throughout the nation; and
14
15 WHEREAS, in 1977, Sidney Moncrief and his teammates won the Southwest
16 Conference Championship, and in 1978, the team made it to the Final Four
17 bracket of the National Collegiate Athletic Association Championship; and
18
19 WHEREAS, in 1979, after the Razorbacks lost a painfully close game to
20 Indiana State University at the National Collegiate Athletic Association
21 Championship, Larry Bird of the Indiana State University team, later to be
22 famed as a power forward for the Boston Celtics, praised Sidney Moncrief as
23 having strength, perfect form, and a great understanding of the game of
24 basketball; and
25
26 WHEREAS, Sidney Moncrief was the first basketball player in University
27 of Arkansas at Fayetteville history to have his jersey number retired, and,
28 along with Corliss Williamson, is one (1) of only two (2) basketball players
29 in University of Arkansas at Fayetteville history ever to be so honored; and
30
31 WHEREAS, after graduating from the University of Arkansas at
32 Fayetteville in 1979 with a Bachelor of Science in physical education, Sidney
33 Moncrief entered the National Basketball Association draft and was chosen
34 fifth overall and as the first-round draft pick of the Milwaukee Bucks; and
35
36 WHEREAS, during his ten (10) years playing for the Milwaukee Bucks,
2 02/05/2025 3:01:56 PM ADF024
HR1031
1 Sidney Moncrief averaged sixteen and seven-tenths (16.7) points per game and
2 over fifty percent (50%) in field goal percentage, and he was twice named the
3 National Basketball Association's Defensive Player of the Year; and
4
5 WHEREAS, a Milwaukee newspaper poll overwhelmingly ranked Sidney
6 Moncrief as the favorite Milwaukee Bucks player of all time, beating out even
7 such legendary players as Kareem Abdul-Jabbar and Oscar Robertson; and
8
9 WHEREAS, legendary professional basketball player Michael Jordan said
10 of Sidney Moncrief, "He'll hound you everywhere you go, both ends of the
11 court. You just expect it"; and
12
13 WHEREAS, after coming out of retirement to play one (1) season with the
14 Atlanta Hawks before fully retiring from the National Basketball Association,
15 Sidney Moncrief bought Buick dealerships in North Little Rock and Pine Bluff
16 and also worked for the Dallas Mavericks, the Golden State Warriors, and the
17 Milwaukee Bucks in various coaching capacities; and
18
19 WHEREAS, in his professional life after his professional basketball
20 career, Sidney Moncrief also operates the Back 2 Basics All-Star Basketball
21 Academy for youth and coaches to provide skills and wellness training and was
22 the head coach of the Fort Worth Flyers of the National Basketball
23 Developmental League as well as the head coach at the University of Arkansas
24 at Little Rock; and
25
26 WHEREAS, Sidney Moncrief has also been part of the broadcast team for
27 the Milwaukee Bucks for Fox Sports Network Wisconsin, and in 2017 he and his
28 wife launched the Moncrief Career Professional Development Center in Little
29 Rock; and
30
31 WHEREAS, Sidney Moncrief was elected to the Arkansas Sports Hall of
32 Fame in 1993, the Wisconsin Athletic Hall of Fame in 1998, and the Naismith
33 Memorial Basketball Hall of Fame in 2019; and
34
35 WHEREAS, while attending the University of Arkansas at Fayetteville,
36 Sidney Moncrief married his wife Debra with whom he has four (4) sons; and
3 02/05/2025 3:01:56 PM ADF024
HR1031
1
2 WHEREAS, Sidney Moncrief is renowned as an Arkansas community and
3 professional leader, brought great renown to the State of Arkansas through
4 his mastery of the game of basketball, and remains one of the most beloved
5 and respected citizens of the State of Arkansas,
6
7 NOW THEREFORE,
8 BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE NINETY-FIFTH GENERAL
9 ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF ARKANSAS:
10
11 THAT the House of Representatives recognize Sidney Moncrief for his
12 contributions to his community, the State of Arkansas, and the nation.
13
14 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED THAT upon adoption, a copy of this resolution be
15 provided to Sidney Moncrief by the Chief Clerk of the House of
16 Representatives.
