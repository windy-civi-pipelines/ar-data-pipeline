17 TO CELEBRATE BLACK HISTORY MONTH DURING
18 FEBRUARY 2025, AND TO RECOGNIZE AND
19 APPLAUD THE SACRIFICES AND MYRIAD
20 CONTRIBUTIONS MADE BY AFRICAN AMERICAN
21 INDIVIDUALS THROUGHOUT THE HISTORY OF
22 ARKANSAS AND THE UNITED STATES.
23
24 WHEREAS, throughout the history of the United States, truly significant
25 African American contributions to social growth, the economy, the military,
26 the sciences, and the arts of this great nation have too often been ignored;
27 and
28
29 WHEREAS, in recognition of this oversight, Dr. Carter Woodson, a
30 distinguished Black author, editor, publisher, and historian initiated the
31 observance of Negro History Week in 1926, and this was the predecessor to the
32 modern celebration of Black History Month and its accompanying focus on the
33 study of African American history; and
34
35 WHEREAS, Black History Month has been celebrated annually since
36 February 1976 and was inspired by the February birthdays of Abraham Lincoln

1 and Frederick Douglass; and
2
3 WHEREAS, the progress of African Americans in the nation and in the
4 State of Arkansas has been marked by both the tragedy of social inequalities
5 and the triumph of group and individual achievements; and
6
7 WHEREAS, although African Americans have participated in every United
8 States war, the battle for the integration of the United States Armed Forces
9 and for recognition of the accomplishments of African American soldiers has
10 been a slow process; and
11
12 WHEREAS, not until after World War II did the United States Armed
13 Forces become integrated under a 1948 executive order by President Harry S.
14 Truman; and
15
16 WHEREAS, Black History Month recognizes commonly celebrated individuals
17 such as Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr., George Washington Carver, Thurgood
18 Marshall, Rosa Parks, Jackie Robinson, and the Little Rock Nine, but also
19 brings to light information to educate all Americans about lesser-known but
20 equally impactful individuals such as Crispus Attucks, William Harvey Carney,
21 the Greensboro Four, Constance Baker Motley, and a plethora of other
22 historical giants; and
23
24 WHEREAS, Dorothy McFadden Hoover of Hope was one of the National
25 Aeronautics and Space Administration's human computers, a physicist and
26 mathematician who developed the celebrated "thin sweptback tapered wing" that
27 revolutionized flight; and
28
29 WHEREAS, Maya Angelou, who spent much of her childhood in Stamps, was a
30 renowned author and poet and one (1) of the first African American women to
31 have a screenplay produced as a feature film; and
32
33 WHEREAS, Maya Angelou's poem, "On the Pulse of the Morning", was
34 delivered at President William Jefferson "Bill" Clinton's inauguration, and
35 she authored the famed and distinguished book, "I Know Why the Caged Bird
36 Sings"; and
2 01/30/2025 11:42:33 AM ADF042
HR1024
1
2 WHEREAS, Revoyda Frierson of Hope grew to fame as Ketty Lester, a
3 singer and actress whose recording "Love Letters" rose to number five (5) on
4 the popular music charts, and who is best known for her role as Hester on the
5 television show "Little House on the Prairie"; and
6
7 WHEREAS, John H. Johnson was a native of Arkansas City, although his
8 family eventually moved to Chicago because there was no high school for
9 African Americans in his hometown, and he became the most influential African
10 American publisher in American history, helming publications such as Ebony
11 Magazine, Jet Magazine, and the Negro Digest; and
12
13 WHEREAS, Raye Montague was a native of Little Rock whose work
14 revolutionized the way the United States Navy designed ships after she
15 developed a computer program that created rough drafts of ship specifications
16 that allowed the United States Navy to decrease the time it took to build a
17 ship's draft design from two (2) years to eighteen (18) hours and twenty-six
18 (26) minutes,
19
20 NOW THEREFORE,
21 BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE NINETY-FIFTH GENERAL
22 ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF ARKANSAS:
23
24 THAT the House of Representatives celebrate Black History Month during
25 February 2025 and recognize and applaud the sacrifices and myriad
26 contributions made by African Americans throughout the history of the great
27 State of Arkansas and the United States.
