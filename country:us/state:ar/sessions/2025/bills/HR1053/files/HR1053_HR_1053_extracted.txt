23 WHEREAS, Zeta Phi Beta Sorority, Incorporated and Phi Beta Sigma
24 Fraternity, Inc., are forever linked by their sororal and fraternal bonds to
25 each other and by their mutual devotion to uplifting their members' lives and
26 serving their communities; and
27
28 WHEREAS, Zeta Phi Beta Sorority, Incorporated was founded January 16,
29 1920, at Howard University in Washington, D.C., during a time of often
30 violent racial turmoil in the nation but also the Harlem Renaissance, one of
31 the first important domestic movements of Black artists and writers in the
32 United States; and
33
34 WHEREAS, Zeta Phi Beta Sorority, Incorporated serves and advocates for
35 health justice both domestically and globally and remains poised for
36 perpetual service to mankind into its second century of existence and beyond;

3 WHEREAS, the members of Zeta Phi Beta Sorority, Incorporated uphold the
4 sorority's ideals of Scholarship, Service, Sisterhood, and Finer Womanhood;

6
7 WHEREAS, Zeta Phi Beta Sorority, Incorporated has eight hundred
8 seventy-five (875) chapters in the United States, two thousand five hundred
9 (2,500) Friends of Zeta, and over two thousand (2,000) youth auxiliaries; and
10
11 WHEREAS, the members of Zeta Phi Beta Sorority, Incorporated encourage
12 achievement of academic excellence by all members and have given three
13 million five hundred thousand dollars ($3,500,000) to college students over
14 the past five (5) years as well as the annual Pearl Anna Neal Scholarship,
15 given every year since 2021 in the amount of one hundred twenty-five thousand
16 dollars ($125,000); and
17
18 WHEREAS, Zeta Phi Beta Sorority, Incorporated is a community-conscious,
19 action-oriented organization dedicated to transforming the lives of others,
20 and has positively impacted over five million (5,000,000) people's lives via
21 its one hundred fifty thousand (150,000) Z-Hope Programs and donations
22 totaling more than eight million dollars ($8,000,000); and
23
24 WHEREAS, Zeta Phi Beta Sorority, Incorporated was the first National
25 Pan-Hellenic Council organization to centralize its operations in a national
26 headquarters, to charter a chapter in Africa, to form auxiliary groups, and
27 to be constitutionally bound to a fraternity, Phi Beta Sigma Fraternity,
28 Inc.; and
29
30 WHEREAS, members of Zeta Phi Beta Sorority, Incorporated have included
31 author Zora Neale Hurston, honorary members and singers Dionne Warwick and
32 Chaka Khan, honorary members and actresses Vivica A. Fox and Tatyana Ali, and
33 honorary member and attorney Anita Hill; and
34
35 WHEREAS, Phi Beta Sigma Fraternity, Inc., has been empowering
36 generations of men since its founding at Howard University in Washington,

3 WHEREAS, the founding of Phi Beta Sigma Fraternity, Inc., was based on
4 a desire to judge each potential member on his own merits rather than his
5 appearance or family connections and also a desire for the fraternity to
6 serve as a mechanism to deliver services to the general community, thereby

10 WHEREAS, the members of Phi Beta Sigma Fraternity, Inc., uphold the

15 (599) active chapters; and

17 WHEREAS, members of Phi Beta Sigma Fraternity, Inc., have been
18 instrumental in the establishment of the Phi Beta Sigma National Foundation
19 and the Phi Beta Sigma Federal Credit Union; and
20
21 WHEREAS, the members of Phi Beta Sigma Fraternity, Inc., assisted with
22 the 1920 founding of its sister organization, Zeta Phi Beta Sorority,
23 Incorporated; and

25 WHEREAS, the members of Phi Beta Sigma Fraternity, Inc., give five
26 hundred seventy-six thousand (576,000) volunteer hours annually, mentor four
27 thousand five hundred (4,500) young men annually, give one million two
28 hundred thousand dollars ($1,200,000) in scholarships to students annually,
29 and have invested eighteen million dollars ($18,000,000) in the Black Spend
30 Initiative; and
31

5 NOW THEREFORE,
6 BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE NINETY-FIFTH GENERAL

8
9 THAT the House of Representatives recognize and honor Zeta Phi Beta
10 Sorority, Incorporated and Phi Beta Sigma Fraternity, Inc., for their
11 contributions to their communities, the State of Arkansas, the nation, and
12 the world.
13
14 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED THAT upon adoption, a copy of this resolution be
15 provided to the leaders of Zeta Phi Beta Sorority, Incorporated and Phi Beta
16 Sigma Fraternity, Inc., by the Chief Clerk of the House of Representatives.
