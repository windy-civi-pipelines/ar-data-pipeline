13 TO RECOGNIZE MARCH 9-15, 2025, AS
14 AMERICORPS WEEK.
15
16 WHEREAS, service to others is a hallmark of the American character and
17 has the unique ability to bring people of all backgrounds together in common
18 cause to meet the nation's most pressing challenges; and
19
20 WHEREAS, thirty-one (31) years ago, Congress established AmeriCorps,
21 formerly the Corporation for National and Community Service, to unite
22 national service programs under one (1) agency, create service opportunities
23 that strengthen communities, and expand pathways to education, careers, and
24 civic participation for those who serve; and
25
26 WHEREAS, AmeriCorps provides opportunities for two hundred thousand
27 (200,000) Americans each year to serve their country through an intensive
28 commitment to service at nonprofits, schools, public agencies, and community
29 and faith-based groups across the country; and
30
31 WHEREAS, national service in AmeriCorps represents a robust public-
32 private partnership that invests in community solutions and leverages
33 nonfederal resources to strengthen community impact and increase the return
34 on taxpayer dollars, resulting in thirty seven million seven hundred thousand
35 dollars ($37,700,000) in AmeriCorps education scholarships earned by
36 Arkansans since 1994; and

2 WHEREAS, in Arkansas, more than three thousand one hundred (3,100)
3 AmeriCorps members and AmeriCorps Seniors volunteers of varying ages and
4 backgrounds helped to meet local needs at more than three hundred (300)
5 locations in the state by responding to disasters, mentoring children and
6 youth, supporting veterans and military families, fighting the opioid
7 epidemic, restoring the environment, addressing food insecurity, and helping
8 secure jobs and housing; and
9
10 WHEREAS, AmeriCorps members and AmeriCorps Seniors volunteers encourage
11 collaboration and partnerships, leveraging millions of volunteers in service
12 and acquiring the support of businesses, foundations, and other local
13 partners to increase the effectiveness of their initiatives; and
14
15 WHEREAS, AmeriCorps and AmeriCorps Seniors programs strengthen bridge-
16 building skills by bringing people together from different backgrounds,
17 generations, and geographic locations to address critical issues facing the
18 country, forging relationships, cultivating mutual respect, and helping to
19 build resilient and thriving communities; and
20
21 WHEREAS, AmeriCorps enriches the lives of its members, building habits
22 that create engaged citizens who are active participants in civic matters
23 affecting their communities long after their service terms are completed; and
24
25 WHEREAS, volunteering prepares AmeriCorps members for future careers
26 and improves the mental well-being of AmeriCorps Seniors volunteers, and
27 their commitment to service reflects the dedication and patriotism of those
28 who serve; and
29
30 WHEREAS, through their service, AmeriCorps members and AmeriCorps
31 Seniors volunteers develop valuable work skills, earn money for continuing
32 education, improve the lives of their families, strengthen communities, and
33 foster civic engagement in Arkansas as a whole; and
34
35 WHEREAS, AmeriCorps Week is an opportunity to recognize the dedication
36 and commitment of two hundred thousand (200,000) AmeriCorps members and

1 AmeriCorps Seniors volunteers who serve each year and the more than one
2 million three hundred thousand (1,300,000) AmeriCorps alumni and millions of
3 AmeriCorps Seniors volunteers and their community partners, and to encourage
4 more Americans to follow in their footsteps in service,
5
6 NOW THEREFORE,
7 BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE NINETY-FIFTH GENERAL
8 ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF ARKANSAS:
9
10 THAT the House of Representatives recognize March 9-15, 2025, as
11 AmeriCorps Week in Arkansas.
12
13 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED THAT upon adoption, a copy of this resolution be
14 provided to the leadership of AmeriCorps in Arkansas by the Chief Clerk of
15 the House of Representatives.
