15 TO ESTABLISH THE ARKANSAS RURAL
16 PRECEPTORSHIP PROGRAM ACT.
17
18 BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF ARKANSAS:
19
20 SECTION 1. Arkansas Code Title 6, Chapter 60, is amended to add an
21 additional subchapter to read as follows:
22 Subchapter 16 — Arkansas Rural Preceptorship Program Act
23
24 6-60-1601. Title.
25 This subchapter shall be known as the “Arkansas Rural Preceptorship
26 Program Act”.
27
28 6-61-1602. Legislative findings and intent.
29 (a) The General Assembly finds that there is a shortage of physicians
30 practicing within rural areas of the state.
31 (b) The General Assembly intends to support the creation of the
32 Arkansas Rural Preceptorship Program, which will:
33 (1) Combine the resources of public and private organizations;
34 (2) Introduce medical students to a rural community to let the
35 medical students experience the benefits of a rural community while raising a
36 family and practicing medicine;
*CRH289*

SB504
1 (3) Use a public-private partnership arrangement to assist rural
2 communities in attracting new physicians to the rural communities; and
3 (4) Provide funding for preceptorship programs in rural
4 communities.
5
6 6-60-1603. Arkansas Rural Preceptorship Program.
7 (a)(1) The Arkansas Rural Preceptorship Program is established to help
8 provide funding for preceptorship programs in rural communities throughout
9 the state.
10 (2) The program established under subdivision (a)(1) of this
11 section shall provide an opportunity for medical students to be placed in a
12 preceptorship at any participating clinic, hospital, federal qualified health
13 center, or other healthcare facility.
14 (b) The following institutions shall be eligible to participate in the
15 program:
16 (1) The University of Arkansas for Medical Sciences;
17 (2) The Arkansas College of Osteopathic Medicine;
18 (3) The New York Institute of Technology College of Osteopathic
19 Medicine at Arkansas State University; and
20 (4) The Alice L. Walton School of Medicine.
21 (c) The Division of Higher Education shall:
22 (1) Administer the program; and
23 (2) Release funding at the per diem rate to a participating
24 institution under subdivision (b) of this section to reimburse the
25 institution for any participating medical student for up to three (3) weeks
26 of placement in the program.
27 (d) Funding for the program under this section may be provided from
28 public or private funds.
