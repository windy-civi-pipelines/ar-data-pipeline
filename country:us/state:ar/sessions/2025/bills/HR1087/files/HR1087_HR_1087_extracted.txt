14 TO RECOGNIZE THE DE QUEEN HIGH SCHOOL
15 LADY LEOPARDS BASKETBALL TEAM AS THE
16 CLASS 4A STATE CHAMPIONS.
17
18 WHEREAS, the De Queen High School Lady Leopards basketball team won the
19 Class 4A state championship held at Bank OZK Arena in Hot Springs, Arkansas,
20 on March 14, 2025; and
21
22 WHEREAS, this was the second state championship title for the Lady
23 Leopards, and their first since 2003; and
24
25 WHEREAS, the Lady Leopards defeated the previously undefeated Pulaski
26 Academy Lady Bruins by a score of sixty-two (62) to fifty-four (54) to win
27 the state championship; and
28
29 WHEREAS, the Lady Leopards finished the season with a record of thirty-
30 five wins and one loss (35-1), setting a record for the most wins in a single
31 season in school history; and
32
33 WHEREAS, the Lady Leopards had fifteen (15) steals and forced twenty
34 (20) turnovers in the championship game, and they converted these into twenty
35 (20) points; and
36

1 WHEREAS, Lady Leopards team members are Azulenny Ponce, Landri Liggin,
2 Lynli Liggin, Jessie Lyle, Shalyn Lyle, Ellie Morphew, Brielle Cooper, Karlie
3 Bush, Tegan Gallardo, Kate Lindsey, Kailynn Klitz, Layken Young, Emma
4 Chambers, and Heavenly Picking along with managers Citlally Ruiz, Denise
5 Hernandez, and Nadelyn Ruiz; and
6
7 WHEREAS, team member Shalyn Lyle was named the state championship's
8 Most Valuable Player; and
9
10 WHEREAS, the Lady Leopards were led by Head Coach Bret Burgess and
11 Assistant Coach Jacie Engler; and
12
13 WHEREAS, Head Coach Bret Burgess said of the victory, "They bought into
14 the plan and worked hard to make it happen [with] defense, effort, and guts",
15
16 NOW THEREFORE,
17 BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE NINETY-FIFTH GENERAL
18 ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF ARKANSAS:
19
20 THAT the House of Representatives recognize the De Queen High School
21 Lady Leopards basketball team as the Class 4A state champions.
22
23 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED THAT upon adoption, a copy of this resolution be
24 presented to Head Coach Bret Burgess by the Chief Clerk of the House of
25 Representatives.
