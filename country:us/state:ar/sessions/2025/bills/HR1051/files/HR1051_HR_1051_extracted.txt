16 TO RECOGNIZE EATING DISORDER ACTION WEEK
17 AS A TIME TO RAISE AWARENESS, PROMOTE
18 EDUCATION, AND ENCOURAGE EARLY
19 INTERVENTION AND TREATMENT FOR EATING
20 DISORDERS.
21
22 WHEREAS, eating disorders affect approximately nine percent (9%) of the
23 population of the United States, equating to twenty-eight million, eight
24 hundred thousand (28,800,000) citizens; and
25
26 WHEREAS, eating disorders have the highest mortality rate of any
27 psychiatric illness, with one (1) person dying every fifty-two (52) minutes
28 as a direct consequence of an eating disorder; and
29
30 WHEREAS, women experience eating disorders at more than two (2) times
31 the rate of men, with lifetime prevalence rates estimated at eight and six-
32 tenths percent (8.6%) for women compared to four and seven-hundredths percent
33 (4.07%) for men; and
34
35 WHEREAS, individuals from marginalized communities, military personnel,
36 athletes, and men face unique challenges in diagnosis, treatment, and access

1 to care for eating disorders; and
2
3 WHEREAS, eating disorders show significant links with other mental
4 health conditions including depression, anxiety, post-traumatic stress
5 disorder, and substance use disorders, with a high prevalence of suicidal
6 ideation and suicide attempts among those affected; and
7
8 WHEREAS, young people, including teenagers and college students, are
9 particularly vulnerable to developing disordered eating behaviors, with risk
10 factors such as dieting, body dissatisfaction, and societal pressures
11 contributing to the rise in cases; and
12
13 WHEREAS, eating disorders have a substantial economic and public health
14 impact, with Arkansas ranking thirteenth in the nation for eating disorder
15 prevalence and incurring an estimated forty-two million, four hundred
16 thousand dollars ($42,400,000) in related healthcare costs annually; and
17
18 WHEREAS, in 2021, Arkansas hospitals recorded five hundred sixteen (516)
19 inpatient admissions and one hundred forty (140) emergency department visits
20 where an eating disorder was diagnosed, and more than four thousand five
21 hundred (4,500) Arkansans had nearly thirty thousand (30,000) insurance
22 claims related to an eating disorder diagnosis,
23
24 NOW THEREFORE,
25 BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE NINETY-FIFTH GENERAL
26 ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF ARKANSAS:
27
28 THAT Eating Disorder Action Week be recognized as a time to raise
29 awareness, promote education, and encourage early intervention and treatment
30 for eating disorders.
31
32 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED THAT medical professionals, educators, policymakers,
33 and community organizations work collaboratively to improve access to
34 treatment, reduce stigma, and provide comprehensive support for individuals
35 affected by eating disorders.
