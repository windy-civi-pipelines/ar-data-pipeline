27 SECTION 1. Arkansas Code Title 4, Chapter 88, is amended to add an
28 additional subchapter to read as follows:
29 Subchapter 15 — Governing Unaccredited Representatives Defrauding (GUARD) VA
30 Benefits Act
31
32 4-88-1501. Title.
33 This subchapter shall be known and may be cited as the "Governing
34 Unaccredited Representatives Defrauding (GUARD) VA Benefits Act".
35
36 4-88-1502. Purpose.
*ZRC043*
02/05/2025 4:55:41 PM ZRC043
HB1423
1 The purpose of this subchapter is to provide a cause of action under
2 the laws of this state to prevent unethical conduct and the improper
3 collection of fees by persons advising or assisting an individual with the
4 filing of a claim for disability benefits before the Department of Veterans
5 Affairs or the United States Department of Veterans Affairs.
6
7 4-88-1503. Definitions.
8 As used in this subchapter:
9 (1) "Compensation" means payment of money, a thing of value, or
10 a financial benefit;
11 (2) "Person" means an individual, corporation, business trust,
12 estate, trust, partnership, limited liability company, joint venture, public
13 corporation, or any other legal or for profit commercial entity; and
14 (3) "Veterans' benefits matter" means the preparation,
15 presentation, or prosecution of a claim affecting a person who has filed or
16 expressed an intent to file a claim for a benefit, program, service,
17 commodity, function, or status, the entitlement to which is determined under
18 the laws and regulations administered by the Department of Veterans Affairs
19 or the United States Department of Veterans Affairs pertaining to veterans,
20 their dependents, their survivors, and any other individual eligible for such
21 benefits.
22
23 4-88-1504. Advising or assisting in veterans' benefits matters —
24 Receipt of compensation.
25 (a)(1) A person may not receive compensation for the preparation,
26 presentation, or prosecution of a claim or for advising, consulting, or
27 assisting an individual with regard to a veterans' benefits matter before the
28 Department of Veterans Affairs or the United States Department of Veterans
29 Affairs, except as permitted under Title 38 U.S.C. § 5904, and 38 C.F.R. §
30 14.629.
31 (2) A person may not receive compensation for referring an
32 individual to another person to prepare, present, or prosecute a claim or
33 advise, consult, or assist the individual with a veterans' benefits matter
34 before the Department of Veterans Affairs or the United States Department of
35 Veterans Affairs.
36 (3) Except as provided under subdivision (a)(1) of this section,
2 02/05/2025 4:55:41 PM ZRC043
HB1423
1 this subsection does not prohibit a division of fees between attorneys that
2 is otherwise proper under the American Bar Association's Model Rules of
3 Professional Conduct, Rule 1.5(e).
4 (b) A person who receives compensation for the preparation,
5 presentation, or prosecution of a claim or for advising, consulting, or
6 assisting an individual with regard to a veterans' benefits matter before the
7 Department of Veterans Affairs or the United States Department of Veterans
8 Affairs shall be held to the same ethical standards to which an attorney is
9 held under the Arkansas Rules of Professional Conduct, including without
10 limitation the rules on:
11 (1) Advertising;
12 (2) Solicitation of new clients; and
13 (3) Confidentiality.
14 (c) A violation of this section is a deceptive and unfair trade
15 practice and constitutes a Class A misdemeanor as a violation of the
16 Deceptive Trade Practices Act, § 4-88-101 et seq.
