15 TO RECOGNIZE THE HMONG COMMUNITY OF
16 ARKANSAS AND THE CONTRIBUTIONS OF ITS
17 PEOPLE TO THEIR CULTURE AND TO OUR
18 COMMUNITIES IN THE STATE OF ARKANSAS.
19
20 WHEREAS, the Hmong community is a group of caring and hardworking
21 individuals who have acculturated into the area by becoming productive
22 members of the communities in which they live; and
23
24 WHEREAS, the Hmong community numbers approximately two thousand five
25 hundred (2,500) members in northwest Arkansas, one thousand (1,000) members
26 in Sebastian County, five hundred (500) members each in Pulaski and Pike
27 Counties, and two hundred fifty (250) members in Madison County; and
28
29 WHEREAS, Hmong community members are agriculturists, teachers, law
30 enforcement officers, poultry and livestock producers, and healthcare workers
31 who look for ways to be productive in society while also working to preserve
32 their culture through teaching the next generations about their language,
33 customs, and traditions; and
34
35 WHEREAS, the Hmong New Year celebration marks the end of the harvest
36 festival; and

2 WHEREAS, the Hmong New Year is a huge celebration in which the Hmong
3 people wear traditional embroidered clothing, traditional food is served,
4 traditional games are played, sports competitions are held, dancing and music
5 is performed, and there is courtship between young people; and
6
7 WHEREAS, the Hmong community in northwest Arkansas has a forty-acre
8 property in Summers, Arkansas, that is used for the Hmong New Year
9 celebration and other events, and this is a great place for the community to
10 assemble and live out their mission of preserving and teaching the next
11 generation their cultural heritage; and
12
13 WHEREAS, during the Laotian Civil War, also known as the Secret War,
14 which took place during the time of the Vietnam War, the Hmong people rescued
15 downed United States pilots and ground infantry, protected a United States
16 radar called "Lima Site 85", and attacked North Vietnamese soldiers on the Ho
17 Chi Minh Trail to prevent them from getting to South Vietnam, operations
18 which saved thousands of American and Laotian lives; and
19
20 WHEREAS, when these brave Hmong people were under threat from the
21 advance of the North Vietnamese Army toward the Hmong base at Long Tieng in
22 Laos, Central Intelligence Agency Paramilitary Operations Officer Jerrold B.
23 "Jerry" Daniels organized the air evacuation into Thailand of his liaison,
24 Hmong General Vang Pao, and more than two thousand (2,000) of Vang Pao's
25 brave officers, soldiers, and their families; and
26
27 WHEREAS, thousands more Hmong people then fled across the Mekong River
28 to Thailand and began living in refugee camps there, and because of the Hmong
29 people's brave support of the United States's presence in Laos, Jerrold B.
30 "Jerry" Daniels worked with these refugees to facilitate their safe
31 resettlement in a number of nations, including the United States,
32
33 NOW THEREFORE,
34 BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVE OF THE NINETY-FIFTH GENERAL
35 ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF ARKANSAS:
