24 SECTION 1. DO NOT CODIFY. Title — Legislative intent.
25 (a) This act shall be known and may be cited as the "Strengthening
26 Arkansas Education Act".
27 (b) It is the intent of the General Assembly:
28 (1) That undergraduate students in state-supported institutions
29 of higher education acquire knowledge and skills that enable the students to
30 contribute to their state and country as informed, self-reliant, and civic-
31 minded citizens;
32 (2) To implement a fully transferrable core curriculum for
33 state-supported colleges and universities that ensures a general education
34 that provides students in Arkansas with a shared knowledge of the history,
35 ideals, and institutions of America, along with a proper introduction to
36 logic, reasoning, and communications;

3 SECTION 5. Arkansas Code Title 6, Chapter 61, Subchapter 1, is amended
4 to add an additional section to read as follows:
5 6-61-144. Arkansas Requisite Core Curriculum — Definitions.
6 (a) This section shall be known and may be cited as the “Arkansas
7 Requisite Core Curriculum”.
8 (b) As used in this section:
9 (1) “Minimum general education core curriculum means” the
10 minimum general education core curriculum requirements for associate and
11 baccalaureate degree programs at state-supported institutions of higher
12 education;
13 (2) “Requisite core curriculum" means the fifteen (15) semester
14 credit hours of introductory survey courses as determined by the Division of
15 Higher Education;
16 (3) “State-supported institution of higher education” means a
17 public postsecondary institution or a department in a public postsecondary
18 institution that:
19 (A) Provides:
20 (i) An eligible program of training to prepare a
21 student for gainful employment in a recognized occupation; or
22 (ii) A program leading to an associate degree or a
23 baccalaureate degree; and
24 (B) Is accredited by a recognized accrediting agency or
25 association and has continuously held the accreditation for the number of
26 years required by the division.
27 (c)(1) Beginning with the entering class of fall 2027, the division,
28 in consultation with the state-supported institutions of higher education,
29 shall establish a minimum general education core curriculum that shall:
30 (A) Include the requisite core curriculum; and
31 (B) Apply toward the minimum requirements for associate
32 and baccalaureate degrees at the state-supported institutions of higher
33 education.
34 (2) The general education core curriculum shall be fully
35 transferable among all state-supported institutions of higher education.
36 completed the minimum general education core curriculum as determined by the

1 division.
2 (d) The requisite core curriculum shall include the following fifteen
3 (15) semester credit hours of introductory survey courses:
4 (1) Two (2) three-semester credit hours of oral or written
5 communication designed to develop the ability to organize ideas and to
6 communicate, with clarity, precision, and syntactical maturity;
7 (2) A three-semester credit hour course that cultivates critical
8 thinking, problem-solving, and logical reasoning skills; and
9 (3) A three-semester credit hour course in American history and
10 a three-semester credit hour course in American government, including:
11 (A) Instruction in the essentials of the United States
12 Constitution; and
13 (B) The study of American institutions and ideals.
14 (e) To ensure compliance with this section, the division may establish
15 reporting requirements for state-supported institutions of higher education
16 detailing implementation of the requisite core curriculum.
17 (f) In determining whether a course meets the general education core
18 curriculum requirements, the division may:
19 (1) Exempt Applied Science degrees;
20 (2) Grant credit hour equivalency for competency-based and
21 proficiency-based baccalaureate degree programs; and
22 (3) Allow for substitution of advanced coursework with specified
23 majors, with the exception that American Government and American History are
24 required for every student.
25 (g) The division may promulgate rules to implement this section.

27 SECTION 6. Arkansas Code § 6-61-218 is repealed.
28 6-61-218. Minimum college core — Transferability.
29 To promote a coordinated system of higher education in Arkansas and to
30 assure an orderly and effective development of each of the state-supported
31 institutions of higher education, the Arkansas Higher Education Coordinating
32 Board shall establish in consultation with state-supported institutions of
33 higher education a minimum core of courses which shall apply toward the
34 general education core curriculum requirements for associate and
35 baccalaureate degrees at state-supported institutions of higher education and
36 which shall be fully transferable among all state-supported institutions of

3 SECTION 7. Arkansas Code § 6-61-231(a)(4)(A), concerning the
4 definition of "state minimum core curriculum" related to statewide transfer
5 agreements, is amended to read as follows:
6 (4)(A) “State minimum core curriculum” means sixty (60) semester
7 hours of lower-division coursework that include the thirty-five-hour general
8 education core, major program prerequisites, and elective requirements that
9 satisfy the requirements of an associate of arts degree, an associate of
10 science degree, an associate of arts in teaching degree, and selected
11 baccalaureate degrees at all public institutions of higher education as
12 determined by the Arkansas Higher Education Coordinating Board.
