1 The General Assembly finds that:
2 (1) Government entities have existing requirements for the
3 operators of various modes of commercial transportation to demonstrate
4 English proficiency;
5 (2) A requirement to demonstrate English proficiency is not
6 unique to the operation of a commercial motor vehicle and is required for
7 other modes of commercial transportation;
8 (3) A demonstration of English proficiency is required during
9 the licensure process for a pilot's license and instrument rating under 14
10 C.F.R. Parts 61, 63, 65, and 107; and
11 (4) The International Civil Aviation Organization, of which the
12 United States is a member, requires operators of commercial aircraft to
13 demonstrate English language proficiency.

16 amended to add additional sections to read as follows:
17 27-23-132. Requirement to possess commercial driver license from valid
18 jurisdiction.
19 An individual operating a commercial motor vehicle within this state

23 the Commonwealth of Puerto Rico; or
24 (2) Both:
25 (A) A valid commercial driver license issued by a state,
26 territory, district, or province of Canada or Mexico; and
27 (B) A valid work visa.

33 this state.

35 considered labor.

15 A nondomiciled commercial driver license or nondomiciled commercial

20 27-23-135. English proficiency.
21 An operator of a commercial motor vehicle shall demonstrate proficiency

24 (2) Understand traffic control devices; and

26 (A) Emergency services;
27 (B) Law enforcement; and
28 (C) Other drivers.

11 27-23-137. Criminal use of a commercial motor vehicle.
12 (a) A person commits the offense of criminal use of a commercial motor
13 vehicle if he or she:
14 (1) Is not a citizen of the United States;
15 (2) Does not hold a valid commercial driver license under § 27-
16 23-132; and
17 (3) Causes the bodily harm of another person while operating a
18 commercial motor vehicle.
19 (b) The offense of criminal use of a commercial motor vehicle is a
20 Class D felony with a minimum term of imprisonment of six (6) months.
21 (c) An offense under this section is a strict liability offense.
22
23 27-23-138. Vehicular homicide with a commercial motor vehicle.
24 (a) A person commits the offense of vehicular homicide with a
25 commercial motor vehicle if he or she:
26 (1) Is not a citizen of the United States;
27 (2) Does not hold a valid commercial driver license under § 27-
28 23-132; and
29 (3) Causes the death of another person while operating a
30 commercial motor vehicle.
31 (b) The offense of vehicular homicide with a commercial motor vehicle
32 is a Class B felony with a minimum term of imprisonment of ten (10) years.
33 (c) An offense under this section is a strict liability offense.
34

36 English language proficiency.

11 27-23-140. Providing commercial motor vehicle to ineligible operator —
12 Penalties — Definition.
13 (a) A commercial motor carrier commits the offense of providing a
14 commercial motor vehicle to an ineligible operator if the commercial motor

17 (2) Violates § 27-23-133, § 27-23-136, or § 27-23-139.
18 (b) The offense of providing a commercial motor vehicle to an
19 ineligible operator is a violation and is subject to a fine of ten thousand
20 dollars ($10,000).
21 (c) As used in this section, "commercial motor carrier" means the same
22 as used in § 27-14-613.
