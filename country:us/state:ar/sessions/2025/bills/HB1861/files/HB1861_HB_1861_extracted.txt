3 23-117-202. Definitions.

12 23-117-203. Interactive gaming.
13 (a)(1)(A) A casino licensee shall not operate an interactive gaming
14 system in this state unless the interactive gaming system has been approved
15 by the Arkansas Racing Commission.
16 (B) An activity conducted under Arkansas Constitution,
17 Amendment 84, shall not be considered interactive gaming.
18 (2) An interactive gaming system operated by a casino licensee
19 is subject to rules promulgated by the commission.
20 (3) A casino licensee is limited to one (1) branded interactive
21 gaming system that is identified with the casino licensee.
22 (4) The operation of interactive gaming is prohibited in
23 circumstances in which a majority of the net casino gaming receipts, as
24 defined by The Arkansas Casino Gaming Amendment of 2018, Arkansas
25 Constitution, Amendment 100, from interactive gaming is paid to a third-party
26 vendor assisting in the operation of the interactive gaming.
27 (b) An interactive gaming system shall meet applicable minimum
28 technical standards provided in Gaming Laboratories International's GLI-19:
29 Standards for Interactive Gaming Systems, as revised on July 17, 2020, or an
30 equivalent certification issued by a testing laboratory as approved and
31 licensed by the commission.
32 (c) A casino licensee may operate a name, image, or likeness drawing
33 game as approved by the commission.
34
35 23-117-204. Internal controls for casino licensees related to
36 interactive gaming.

1 (a) A casino licensee shall comply with:
2 (1) Standards related to interactive gaming adopted by the

5 (b) The commission shall:

16 no less than monthly.

20 23-117-205. Acceptance of wagers.
21 A casino licensee shall not accept or facilitate a wager on interactive
22 gaming other than as set forth by rule of the Arkansas Racing Commission.
23
24 23-117-206. Disposition of dormant accounts.
25 (a)(1) A casino licensee may suspend a dormant account.
26 (2) The casino licensee may reactivate a dormant account
27 suspended under subdivision (a)(1) of this section only after reverifying the
28 current government-issued picture identification of the account holder and
29 other information as required by the Arkansas Racing Commission.
30 (b) All amounts remaining inactive in the dormant account after a
31 period of two (2) years of the account's becoming a dormant account shall be
32 paid into a cash fund of the commission.
33 (c) Before closing a dormant account under this section, the casino
34 licensee shall attempt to contact the account holder a minimum of one (1)
35 time per year.

11 SECTION 3. EMERGENCY CLAUSE. It is found and determined by the
12 General Assembly of the State of Arkansas that unlicensed, unregulated, and
13 untaxed casino style and sports wagering offerings are an ever-growing threat
14 to the citizens of Arkansas. Further, institutions of higher education and
15 their affiliated nonprofit organizations are intertwined with economic
16 development, the education of student-athletes, commerce, and other
17 governmental interests that otherwise impact the State of Arkansas; that a
18 drawing game conducted through interactive gaming, in order to benefit a
19 name, image, or likeness organization, the net proceeds of which, after
20 incurred expenses, are equally divided between a name, image, or likeness
21 organization and the participant, can increase economic development,
22 education, and commerce in the State of Arkansas; and that this act is
23 immediately necessary to ensure that institutions of higher education and
24 their affiliated nonprofit organizations can financially support their
25 student-athletes and athletic programs, which will bolster economic
26 development, education, and commerce in the State of Arkansas. Therefore, an
27 emergency is declared to exist, and this act being immediately necessary for
28 the preservation of the public peace, health, and safety shall become
29 effective on:
30 (1) The date of its approval by the Governor;
31 (2) If the bill is neither approved nor vetoed by the Governor,
32 the expiration of the period of time during which the Governor may veto the
33 bill; or
34 (3) If the bill is vetoed by the Governor and the veto is
35 overridden, the date the last house overrides the veto.
