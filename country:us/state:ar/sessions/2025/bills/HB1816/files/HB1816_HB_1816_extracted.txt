26 SECTION 1. Arkansas Code Title 17, Chapter 80, Subchapter 1, is
27 amended to add an additional section to read as follows:
28 17-80-123. Artificial intelligence in healthcare prohibition —
29 Exemption — Definitions.
30 (a) As used in this section:
31 (1) "Artificial intelligence" means a machine-based system that
32 can, for a given set of human-defined objectives, make predictions,
33 recommendations, or decisions influencing real or virtual environments;
34 (2)(A) "Health benefit plan" means:
35 (i) An individual, blanket, or group plan, or a
36 policy or contract for healthcare services offered, issued, renewed,

2 (ii) A health benefit program receiving state or

4 Medicaid Program and the Arkansas Health and Opportunity for Me Program or

6 (B) "Health benefit plan" includes:
7 (i) Indemnity and managed care plans; and
8 (ii) Nonfederal governmental plans as defined in 29

21 seq., or the Public Employee Workers' Compensation Act, § 21-5-601 et seq.;
22 (vii) A plan that provides only indemnity for
23 hospital confinement;
24 (viii) An accident-only plan;
25 (ix) A specified disease plan; or
26 (x) A long-term-care-only plan;
27 (3)(A) "Healthcare insurer" means an insurance company, hospital
28 and medical service corporation, or health maintenance organization that
29 issues or delivers health benefit plans in this state and is subject to:
30 (i) The insurance laws of this state;
31 (ii) Section 23-75-101 et seq., pertaining to
32 hospital and medical service corporations; or
33 (iii) Section 23-76-101 et seq., pertaining to
34 health maintenance organizations.
35 (B) "Healthcare insurer" does not include an entity that
36 provides only dental benefits or eye and vision care benefits;

1 (4) "Healthcare provider" means an individual or entity that is
2 licensed in this state to provide healthcare services; and
3 (5) "Quality assurance laboratory" means an entity or facility
4 that tests and evaluates the performance of artificial intelligence and
5 ensures that the artificial intelligence meets high standards for accuracy,
6 reliability, safety, and ethical considerations.
7 (b) A healthcare provider or a healthcare insurer shall not use
8 artificial intelligence in the delivery of healthcare services or for the
9 generation of medical records unless the artificial intelligence is approved
10 by the United States Food and Drug Administration and verified by a quality
11 assurance laboratory.
