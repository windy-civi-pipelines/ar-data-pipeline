21 SECTION 1. Arkansas Code Title 10, Chapter 3, is amended to add an

24 Joint Committee on Military and Veterans Affairs
25
26 10-3-3301. Joint Committee on Military and Veterans Affairs – Creation
27 – Members.
28 (a) There is established a joint committee of the House of
29 Representatives and the Senate to be known as the “Joint Committee on
30 Military and Veterans Affairs”.
31 (b)(1) The Joint Committee on Military and Veterans Affairs shall be a
32 select committee of the House of Representatives and a joint committee of the
33 Senate whose members shall be selected as follows:
34 (A)(i) Twelve (12) members of the House of Representatives
35 to be selected as prescribed by the Rules of the House of Representatives.
36 (ii) One (1) alternate member for each of the House

16 Affairs as the House Cochair of the Joint Committee on Military and Veterans

24 Chair of the Joint Committee on Military and Veterans Affairs.

26 10-3-3302. Powers and Duties of Joint Committee on Military and
27 Veterans Affairs.
28 (a) The Joint Committee on Military and Veterans Affairs may meet
29 during:
30 (1) A regular session, fiscal session, or extraordinary session
31 of the General Assembly;
32 (2) An extended recess of the General Assembly; and
33 (3) The interim between legislative sessions of the General
34 Assembly.
35 (b) A bill or resolution introduced during a legislative session in
36 either house of the General Assembly pertaining to military or veterans

1 affairs may be referred to the Joint Committee on Military and Veterans
2 Affairs.
3 (c)(1) The Joint Committee on Military and Veterans Affairs shall make
4 continuing studies concerning issues related to military and veterans
5 affairs, either initiated by the Joint Committee on Military and Veterans
6 Affairs or referred to it by either house of the General Assembly for study.
7 (2) Interim study proposals and resolutions filed with the
8 Legislative Council under § 10-3-214 for review and referral to the
9 appropriate interim committee of the General Assembly, relating to military
10 and veterans affairs, shall be referred to the Joint Committee on Military
11 and Veterans Affairs.
12
13 10-3-3303. Interim committee meetings – Expenses – Staff.
14 (a)(1) Members are entitled to per diem and mileage for attendance at
15 meetings of the Joint Committee on Military and Veterans Affairs at the rate
16 provided by law to be paid from funds appropriated for payment of per diem
17 and mileage for attendance at meetings of interim committees of the House of
18 Representatives and the Senate.
19 (2) The per diem and mileage, including reimbursement for
20 expenses for attending out-of-state meetings as provided by law, shall be
21 paid from funds appropriated for per diem, mileage, and expenses of members
22 of the General Assembly for attending interim committee meetings or from
23 other funds provided by law for that purpose.
24 (b) The Bureau of Legislative Research shall furnish such staff
25 assistance as may be requested by the Joint Committee on Military and
26 Veterans Affairs.
27 (c) All other appropriate state agencies, including without limitation
28 the Department of the Military and the Department of Veterans Affairs, shall
29 be available to assist the Joint Committee on Military and Veterans Affairs
30 on matters before it, as may be requested by the Joint Committee on Military
31 and Veterans Affairs.
32
33 SECTION 2. EMERGENCY CLAUSE. It is found and determined by the
34 General Assembly of the State of Arkansas that the State of Arkansas faces
35 increased and ongoing issues due to the growth of the military presence in
36 the state, that the state must face and address these issues to better
3 01-30-2025 09:49:35 JET007
As Engrossed: S1/30/25 HB1056
1 protect our veterans and their families, that protection of our veterans and
2 their families is paramount to the safety of our state and nation, and that
3 this act is immediately necessary as veterans’ issues are crucial and vital
4 to the State of Arkansas. Therefore, an emergency is declared to exist, and
5 this act being immediately necessary for the preservation of the public
6 peace, health, and safety shall become effective on:
7 (1) The date of its approval by the Governor;
8 (2) If the bill is neither approved nor vetoed by the Governor,
9 the expiration of the period of time during which the Governor may veto the
10 bill; or
11 (3) If the bill is vetoed by the Governor and the veto is
12 overridden, the date the last house overrides the veto.
