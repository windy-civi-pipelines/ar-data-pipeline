14 TO ESTABLISH A STATE EXAMINATION FOR
15 LICENSURE OF MASSAGE THERAPISTS.
16
17 BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF ARKANSAS:
18
19 SECTION 1. Arkansas Code Title 17, Chapter 86, Subchapter 3, is
20 amended to add an additional section to read as follows:
21 17-86-315. State examination for licensure.
22 (a)(1) The Department of Health shall administer a state examination
23 for licensure as a massage therapist that shall be administered at the time
24 and place designated by the department.
25 (2) The state examination under this section is an additional
26 option to licensure in this state and does not replace or negate a national
27 examination.
28 (3) The state examination under this section shall be offered as
29 an optional pathway to licensure and shall only be offered to unlicensed
30 graduates of an approved massage therapy school or students enrolled in a
31 massage therapy apprenticeship program in this state who have completed the
32 educational requirements under ยง 17-86-306(e).
33 (b)(1) The state examination shall be a one hundred (100) question
34 examination and be proportionate to educational requirements and classroom
35 hours based on the Massage Therapy Act, ยง 17-86-101 et seq.
36 (2) The state examination shall include:

1 (A) Nine (9) questions concerning anatomy and physiology;
2 (B) Nine (9) questions concerning kinesiology;
3 (C) Twenty (20) questions concerning pathology,
4 contraindications, and special populations;
5 (D) Forty-five (45) questions concerning massage
6 techniques;
7 (E) Five (5) questions concerning hydrotherapy,
8 electrotherapy, and heliotherapy;
9 (F) Five (5) questions concerning hygiene and infection
10 control; and
11 (G) Seven (7) questions concerning massage therapy law,
12 business management, and personal ethics.
13 (c) A passing grade of seventy percent (70%) on the state examination
14 is required for licensure.
15 (d) The state examination shall not be computer adaptive.
16 (e)(1) The department may require massage therapy instructors who
17 currently provide instruction in an approved massage therapy school in this
18 state or a massage therapy apprenticeship program in this state to submit
19 examination questions for the state examination.
20 (2) An examination question submitted by a massage therapy
21 instructor shall:
22 (A)(i) Be derived from a textbook that the massage therapy
23 instructor is currently utilizing to provide instruction to massage therapy
24 students under subdivision (e) of this section.
25 (ii) Material from the Massage and Bodywork
26 Licensing Examination shall not be considered a textbook under this section;
27 and
28 (B) Include:
29 (i) The title of the textbook;
30 (ii) The author of the textbook;
31 (iii) The edition of the textbook;
32 (iv) The page number and line number from which the
33 examination question was derived or obtained; and
34 (v) The date when the examination question was
35 created.
