27 SECTION 1. Arkansas Code Title 12, Chapter 6, is amended to add an
28 additional subchapter to read as follows:
29 Subchapter 9 — Body-Worn Cameras
30
31 12-6-901. Legislative intent.
32 It is the intent of the General Assembly to ensure that:
33 (1) Law enforcement officers and the public are provided the
34 protection of recorded interactions between law enforcement officers and the
35 public; and
36 (2) An accurate record of interactions between law enforcement

1 officers and the public is available for legal and training purposes.
2
3 12-6-902. Definitions.
4 As used in this subchapter:
5 (1)(A) "Law enforcement agency" means any police department,
6 county sheriff's office, or other public agency, force, or organization whose
7 primary responsibility is the enforcement of the criminal laws, traffic laws,
8 highway laws, or game and fish laws of this state.
9 (B) "Law enforcement agency" includes without limitation a
10 college or university police department; and
11 (2)(A) "Law enforcement officer" means a person that is:
12 (i) Employed by a law enforcement agency or other
13 public entity; and
14 (ii) Vested by law with a duty to maintain public
15 order and make arrests for offenses.
16 (B) "Law enforcement officer" includes without limitation
17 a court bailiff, school resource officer, wildlife enforcement officer, game
18 warden, officer of the State Capitol Police, county sheriff, and chief of
19 police.
20
21 12-6-903. Body-worn cameras required.
22 (a) By December 31, 2026, a law enforcement agency or other public
23 entity that employs a law enforcement officer shall:
24 (1) Provide a body-worn camera to each law enforcement officer
25 the law enforcement agency or other public entity employs;
26 (2) Train each law enforcement officer the law enforcement
27 agency or other public entity employs in the use of a body-worn camera; and
28 (3) Enact policies and procedures governing the use of a body-
29 worn camera.
30 (b) A law enforcement officer shall:
31 (1) Wear a body-worn camera while on duty; and
32 (2) Ensure the body-worn camera records all official interaction
33 between the law enforcement officer and the public while the law enforcement
34 officer is on duty.
35
36 12-6-904. Rulemaking.

1 Any state agency, department, board, or commission that employs a law
2 enforcement officer, including without limitation constitutional agencies,
3 shall promulgate rules to implement this subchapter.
4
5 SECTION 2. DO NOT CODIFY. TEMPORARY LANGUAGE.
6 (a) When adopting the initial rules required under this act, the state
7 agency, department, board, or commission shall file the final rules with the
8 Secretary of State for adoption under § 25-15-204(f):
9 (1) On or before August 1, 2026; or
10 (2) If approval under § 10-3-309 has not occurred by August 1,
11 2026, as soon as practicable after approval under § 10-3-309.
12 (b) The state agency, department, board, or commission shall file the
13 proposed rules with the Legislative Council under § 10-3-309(c) sufficiently
14 in advance of August 1, 2026, so that the Legislative Council may consider
15 the rules for approval before August 1, 2026.
