13 TO CELEBRATE THE SESQUICENTENNIAL OF
14 HOPE, ARKANSAS.
15
16 WHEREAS, Hope, Arkansas, was a settlement founded on the Cairo and
17 Fulton Railroad in 1852, and received its “Hopeful” name in honor of the
18 youngest daughter of the Commissioner of the Cairo and Fulton Railroad; and
19
20 WHEREAS, Hope was incorporated April 8, 1875, and became a bustling and
21 growing community; and
22
23 WHEREAS, Hope has endured and prospered throughout its one hundred
24 fifty (150) years, thriving on the backs of citizens past and present who
25 have and do instill a sense of community pride; and
26
27 WHEREAS, Hope has been the birthplace of many who have achieved
28 prominence in the great State of Arkansas, nationally and internationally,
29 most notably former fortieth and forty-second Governor of Arkansas and former
30 forty-second President of the United States, the Honorable William Jefferson
31 "Bill" Clinton, and the former forty-fourth Governor of Arkansas, the
32 Honorable Michael Dale "Mike" Huckabee; and
33
34 WHEREAS, always striving to improve the quality of life, the citizens
35 of Hope are successfully reviving their downtown, the heartbeat of any city,
36 and recently voted for improvements to the town's parks and the construction

1 of an indoor recreational facility; and
2
3 WHEREAS, collaboration between Hope Public Schools and the University
4 of Arkansas Hope-Texarkana has resulted in more Hope High School seniors

7

10
11 NOW THEREFORE,

14
15 THAT the House of Representatives take great pride in congratulating
16 Hope, Arkansas, on the celebration of its sesquicentennial and its great
17 legacy and hope for the future.
