19 BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF ARKANSAS:
20
21 SECTION 1. Arkansas Code Title 20, Chapter 9, Subchapter 2, is amended
22 to add an additional section to read as follows:
23 20-9-225. Standards for surgical smoke evacuation — Definitions.
24 (a) As used in this section:
25 (1) "Energy-generating device" means a tool that performs a
26 surgical function using heat, laser, electricity, or other form of energy;
27 (2)(A) "Smoke evacuation system" means a smoke evacuator, laser
28 plume evacuator, or local exhaust ventilator that effectively captures and
29 neutralizes surgical smoke at the site of origin and before the surgical
30 smoke can make ocular contact or contact with the respiratory tract of
31 occupants of the room.
32 (B) "Smoke evacuation system" is not required to be
33 interconnected to a surgical ventilation system or medical gas system; and
34 (3) "Surgical smoke" means the gaseous by-product produced by
35 energy-generating devices, including surgical plume, smoke plume, bio-
36 aerosols, laser-generated airborne contaminants, or lung-damaging dust.

1 (b) Any healthcare facility licensed by the Department of Health that
