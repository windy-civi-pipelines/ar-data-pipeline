26 SECTION 1. Arkansas Code § 6-21-120(a), concerning definitions
27 regarding public school restroom and changing area designation requirements,
28 is amended to read as follows:
29 (a) As used in this section:
30 (1) "Athletic event" means an organized athletic activity that
31 is sponsored by or associated with a public school district or an open-
32 enrollment public charter school and in which participants are:
33 (A) Engaged in an athletic game or competition against
34 another team, club, or entity; or
35 (B) In practice or preparation for an organized athletic
36 game or competition against another team, club, or entity;

1 (2) "Athletics personnel" means athletic directors and other
2 individuals actively involved in organizing, training, or coaching athletic
3 events for student athletes;
4 (1)(A)(3)(A) “Multiple occupancy restroom or changing area”
5 means an area in a public school district or open-enrollment public charter
6 school building that is designed or designated to be used by one (1) or more
7 individuals at the same time and in which one (1) or more individuals may be
8 in various stages of undress in the presence of other individuals.
9 (B) “Multiple occupancy restroom or changing area”
10 includes without limitation the following:
11 (i) A restroom;
12 (ii) A locker room;
13 (iii) A changing room; and
14 (iv) A shower room; and
15 (2)(A)(4)(A) “Sex” means the physical condition of being male or
16 female based on genetics and physiology.
17 (B) A public school district or open-enrollment public
18 charter school may rely upon a public school student's sex as identified on
19 his or her original birth certificate issued at or near the time of his or
20 her birth.
21
22 SECTION 2. Arkansas Code § 6-21-120(c), concerning exceptions to the
23 requirement that public school restrooms and changing areas be designated
24 based on sex, is amended to read as follows:
25 (c) This section does not apply to an individual who enters a multiple
26 occupancy restroom or changing area designated for use by the opposite sex
27 when he or she enters for at least one (1) of the following circumstances:
28 (1) For custodial, maintenance, or inspection purposes;
29 (2) To render emergency medical assistance; or
30 (3) To address an ongoing emergency, including without
31 limitation a physical altercation; or
32 (4) The individual is a coach or other athletics personnel
33 member who enters and is present in the multiple occupancy restroom or
34 changing area to address, supervise, or treat student athletes that the
35 individual is overseeing during a reasonable time before an athletic event,
36 during an athletic event, or immediately following an athletic event if:
