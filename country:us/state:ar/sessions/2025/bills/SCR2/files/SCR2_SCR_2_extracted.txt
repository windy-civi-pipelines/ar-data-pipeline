18 WHEREAS, the American Dream has been a beacon of hope for generations;

20
21 WHEREAS, the American Dream embodies the ideals of opportunity,
22 prosperity, and upward mobility, promising that every person should have the
23 chance to achieve what they themselves define as success and fulfillment
24 through hard work and determination; and
25
26 WHEREAS, many today feel as though the American Dream is unattainable;
27 and
28
29 WHEREAS, the American Dream belongs to all of us, not just billionaires
30 and multi-millionaires; and
31
32 WHEREAS, our highest accomplishments as a state and nation have been
33 achieved when we harnessed the strengths of all people regardless of identity
34 to overcome our greatest challenges; and
35
36 WHEREAS, Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion (DEI) is a centuries-old

6 WHEREAS, DEI policies, including the Nineteenth Amendment, the Civil

1
2 WHEREAS, efforts to attack DEI are harmful to the bottom line and
3 health of our economy,
4
5 NOW THEREFORE,
6 BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE NINETY-FIFTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE
7 STATE OF ARKANSAS, THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING THEREIN:
8
9 THAT the State of Arkansas affirms its commitment to DEI as an
10 essential foundation for achieving the American Dream and fostering
11 environments where all individuals have the freedom to be healthy,
12 prosperous, and safe and the opportunity to realize their full potential.
13
14 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED THAT the State of Arkansas encourages local, state,
15 and federal policymakers, educational institutions, workplaces, and other
16 organizations to adopt and uphold DEI principles that promote inclusivity,
17 protect freedom of expression, remove barriers, and provide equitable
18 opportunities for all individuals to pursue their dreams.
