20 WHEREAS, Loretta Cecilia (Pfingstel) Ford, cofounder of the first
21 academic program for nurse practitioners and the first Advanced Practice
22 Registered Nurse, died January 22, 2025, at the age of one hundred four
23 (104); and
24
25 WHEREAS, Loretta Ford was born December 28, 1920, in New York City, New
26 York, and was raised in the Haskell section of Wanaque, New Jersey; and
27
28 WHEREAS, Loretta Ford graduated from Butler High School in Butler, New
29 Jersey, in 1937 and then worked at Middlesex General Hospital, known today as
30 the Robert Wood Johnson University Hospital, in New Brunswick, New Jersey, as
31 a nurse's aid; and
32
33 WHEREAS, Loretta Ford then entered nursing school and obtained her
34 nursing diploma in 1941; and
35
36 WHEREAS, in 1942, after her fiancé was killed in World War II, Loretta

4 WHEREAS, through the G.I. Bill, Loretta Ford then attended the
5 University of Colorado where in 1949 she earned a Bachelor of Science in
6 Nursing with a Public Health Nursing certificate; and
7
8 WHEREAS, Loretta Ford was mentored by influential figures in public
9 health and nursing during her time at the University of Colorado, and in 1951
10 she earned a Master of Public Health degree from the university and became
11 the director of nursing at the Boulder City-County Health Department; and
12
13 WHEREAS, Loretta Ford married William Ford in 1947, and they had a
14 daughter in 1952; and
15
16 WHEREAS, Loretta Ford earned a Doctorate in Education from the
17 University of Colorado in 1961, and she was already an assistant professor at
18 the University of Colorado School of Nursing in Denver, earning a full
19 professorship in 1965; and
20
21 WHEREAS, after noticing gaps in healthcare that she felt could be
22 addressed by specially trained nurses, and working in tandem with
23 pediatrician Dr. Henry Silver, Loretta Ford started the pediatric nurse
24 practitioner program at the University of Colorado in 1965, and this was the
25 first academic program for nurse practitioners in United States history; and
26
27 WHEREAS, in 1972, Loretta Ford became founding dean of the University
28 of Rochester School of Nursing in New York, where she developed the holistic
29 Unification Model of Nursing and continued to work to overcome prejudice
30 regarding the abilities of nurses; and
31
32 WHEREAS, Loretta Ford received numerous awards and recognitions
33 throughout her lifetime, including awards from the American Public Health
34 Association, the National Academy of Medicine, and the American Academy of
35 Nursing, and she was inducted into the National Women's Hall of Fame and the
36 Colorado Women's Hall of Fame,
